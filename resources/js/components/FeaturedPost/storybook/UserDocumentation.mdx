# Featured Post

The **Featured Post** block highlights a single editorial post with a strong visual hierarchy, combining a large featured image, supporting post metadata, and a contextual callout section.

It is designed to promote **high-priority or editorial content** such as news, announcements, or featured articles.

---

## When to Use This Block

Use the Featured Post block when:

- You want to highlight a single important article or post
- You need a strong visual + editorial storytelling layout
- You want to combine post content with a promotional context
- You are featuring news, announcements, or editorial highlights

### Example uses:

- Featured blog post on a homepage
- Editorial highlight in a landing page
- News spotlight section
- Campaign or announcement feature
- “Latest story” promotion

## Block Structure

The block is composed of three main visual areas:

- Caption (floating label)
- Featured post (image + post content)
- Context callout (supporting message and CTA)

---

## Heading & Paragraph

The top-level content introduces the featured section.

### Heading (required)

The main title of the featured post section.

- Used as the primary editorial headline
- Appears in the contextual CTA area

---

### Paragraph (required)

Supporting description that introduces or frames the featured content.

- Used to provide editorial context
- Typically short-form copy

---

## Caption

A short label displayed in the upper-right area of the block.

- Acts as a category or section label (e.g. “Latest News”, “Featured Story”)
- Rendered in uppercase styled text
- Positioned above or alongside the featured image area

---

## Buttons

Optional call-to-action buttons linked to the main context.

Examples:

- “Read more”
- “View all posts”
- “Explore”

Buttons are passed through a cloned button group field.

---

## Featured Post Content

The **post object** contains the main featured content.

### Image

- Large visual asset for the featured post
- Acts as the primary visual anchor
- Required for proper layout rendering

---

### Post Context

Includes structured content about the post:

#### Subheading

- Typically the post date or metadata

#### Heading

- Title of the post

#### Paragraph

- Short excerpt or summary of the post

#### Buttons

- Optional CTAs related to the post itself

---

## Layout Behavior

### Desktop Layout

- 12-column grid layout
- Featured image spans majority of left area
- Context block positioned on the right
- Caption is positioned absolutely in the top-right region

---

### Caption Positioning

- Positioned over or near the featured image area
- Includes decorative animated border element
- Acts as a visual label for the section

---

## Ordering

On desktop:

1. Featured image (primary focus)
2. Post summary content (under image)
3. Context CTA (right column)
4. Caption overlay (top-right)

---

## Tips for Best Results

- The post image is required
- Heading, paragraph, and caption are required for full layout integrity
- Best used for **single-item promotion only**
- Avoid using multiple Featured Post blocks on the same page
- Works best when paired with strong editorial imagery
