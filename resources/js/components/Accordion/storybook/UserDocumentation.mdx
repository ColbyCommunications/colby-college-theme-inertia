# Accordion

The **Accordion** block lets you display a list of expandable sections that visitors can open and close.

Each section contains a **heading** and **content**. Visitors click the heading to expand or collapse the panel.

---

## When to Use This Block

Use the Accordion when:

- You have multiple related pieces of content
- You want to keep pages shorter and easier to scan
- You want visitors to reveal information only when they need it

### Example uses:

- FAQ sections
- Program/course details
- Policies

## Block Settings

### Single

If enabled, **only one accordion panel can be open at a time**.

- Opening a new panel will automatically close the currently open one.
- This is commonly used for **FAQ sections**.

If disabled, visitors can open **multiple panels at once**.

---

### Open by Default

If enabled, the **first accordion panel will be open automatically when the page loads**.

Use this if:

- The first panel contains important information
- You want to highlight the first item

If disabled, all panels start **closed**.

---

## Panels

Panels are the individual expandable sections inside the accordion.

You can add as many panels as you need.

Each panel includes:

### Heading

The **title of the accordion section**.

This is what visitors click to open the panel.

---

### Content

The **content that appears when the panel is opened**.

You can add:

- Text
- Lists
- Links
- Basic formatting (bold, italics, etc.)

For more complex content (nested blocks), use the **Advanced Accordion**.

---

## Tips for Best Results

- Keep headings **short and scannable**.
- Avoid placing **very large amounts of content** inside one panel.
- Use **Single mode** for FAQ sections.
- Use **multiple-open mode** when users may want to compare information between panels.
